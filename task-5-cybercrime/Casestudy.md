Case Study: The Ransomware Attack on Jaguar Land Rover (2025).

Ransomware is a form of malware that encrypts a victim's files, making them inaccessible until a "ransom" (usually in cryptocurrency) is paid. In modern "double extortion" schemes, attackers also steal sensitive data and threaten to leak it publicly.

How the Crime Happened (Step-by-Step)
According to reports from SOCRadar and Industrial Cyber, the attack on Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) in 2025 followed a sophisticated lifecycle:
1.	Initial Breach (March 2025): The "Hellcat" ransomware group gained access to JLR’s systems, likely through exploited software vulnerabilities or stolen credentials.
2.	Data Exfiltration: Before encrypting anything, they quietly stole over 350GB of sensitive data, including source code and employee records.
3.	Active Intrusion (August 2025): The attackers moved laterally through the network to plant the ransomware payload.
4.	Operational Shutdown: To contain the spread, JLR was forced to shut down its global IT environment.
5.	Extortion: The attackers demanded payment to unlock files and prevent the leaked data from being sold.
Who was Targeted?
The primary target was a large-scale manufacturing corporation. Cybercriminals frequently target the manufacturing and critical infrastructure sectors because these organizations cannot afford long periods of downtime, providing the attackers with maximum leverage.
The Consequences
The impact was staggering. Production was paralyzed for nearly a month across plants in the UK, Slovakia, China, and India, resulting in a manufacturing loss of approximately 1,000 vehicles per day. Beyond the massive revenue loss and recovery costs—estimated as one of Britain’s costliest cyberattacks—the company faced significant reputational damage and the exposure of sensitive internal intellectual property.

Source: SOCRadar, "Top 10 Ransomware Attacks of 2025"; Industrial Cyber, "Half of 2025 ransomware attacks hit critical sectors."
